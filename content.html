Prioritizing Safety over Poverty

This issue is mainly caused by heightening levels of population growth. Hunger and malnutrition statistic levels peaks due to a lack of money and resources among poverty-stricken people as resources lessen due to high population growth. Also, this results in impoverished people who spend too much money, not having the ability to support the increasing number of members of their families, and the inability to afford housing due to lack of employment and education. Furthermore, GDP per capita is decreasing as the number of poverty-stricken people increases. Moreover, overpopulation is not the only issue that impacted poverty. Economic decline and corruptions are also factors that maintain the burning rage of poverty. We need a solution to diminish all the factors of poverty in just a blink of the eye. We need as well as a solution that does not require an enormous amount of funds to implement. All of these together will formulate a solution that will benefit everyone as they may profit from it.
